The Red-Crowned Crane


The red-crowned crane is one of the rarest and most elegant birds in the world. Known for its striking appearance and graceful movements, it often captures people's imagination. Even in Japan, where it is called tanchōzuru (丹頂鶴) is celebrated as a symbol of luck, longevity, and fidelity.

The word tanchōzuru is made up of tree parts in japanese:

tan (丹):
refers to the red color.
chō (頂):
indicate the top of the head.
tsuru (鶴):
means crane, the kind of bird.

Physical Traits and Key Facts

Description

  • Can grow up to 1.5 meters (5ft) tall, with wingspan about 2.5 meters(8ft)
  • Distinctive red crown on the head
  • White body with black neck and wings
  • Lifespan: up to 40 years in the wild

Diet

  • Rice and grains
  • Small fish
  • Frogs
  • Insects
  • Aquatic plants

Soundscape of red-crowned crane "The trumpets call"

The Dance of the Red-Crowned Crane

The red-crowned crane is monogamous, likely mates for life, and reaches breeding maturity at 3-4 years. Mating and egg-laying mainly occur in April and early May.

Pairs perform duets and dances to strengthen their bond, signal territory, and communicate with other cranes. Duet displays typically begin with a male call, and both birds move and call in unison. Dancing also signals excitement and helps maintain presence in favorable areas, supporting joint resource defense.

Fame and Symbolism

They are famous for their courtship dance, where pairs bow and leap in unison. This ritual resembles the elegance of snow ballerinas performing on stage.

It's symbolism stems from a myth that cranes can live for 1,000 years, making them a national treasure and a cherished cultural icon, often depicted in art, literature, and on important garments like wedding kimonos.



The current Japan Airlines logo proudly displays a red-crowned crane. As designer J. Huff explained, I had faith that it was the perfect symbol for Japan Air Lines. I found that the crane myth was all positive—it mates for life (loyalty), and flies high for miles without tiring (strength).
